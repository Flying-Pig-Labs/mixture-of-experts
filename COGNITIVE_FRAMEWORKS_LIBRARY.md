## Strategic Planning Frameworks

### VRIO Analysis
**Purpose:** Assess competitive advantage potential of resources and capabilities

**Components:**
- **V (Value):** Does it create value?
- **R (Rarity):** Is it rare in the industry?
- **I (Inimitability):** Is it hard to copy?
- **O (Organization):** Can you organize to exploit it?

**How to Use:** Evaluate each major resource/capability through VRIO lens

**Best For:** Competitive advantage analysis, resource strategy

---

### The Value Stick
**Purpose:** Visualize value creation between customer willingness to pay and supplier costs

**Components:**
- Willingness to Pay (WTP) - top
- Price
- Cost
- Willingness to Sell (WTS) - bottom
- Gaps: Customer Delight, Firm Margin, Supplier Surplus

**How to Use:**
- Enhance WTP: Increase customer perceived value
- Lower WTS: Reduce cost structure

**Best For:** Pricing strategy, margin optimization, value chain analysis

---

### Blue Ocean Strategy
**Purpose:** Create uncontested market space rather than compete in saturated markets

**Four Actions Framework:**
- **Eliminate:** What factors can we remove that the industry has long competed on?
- **Reduce:** What can we reduce well below industry standard?
- **Raise:** What should we raise well above industry standard?
- **Create:** What factors should we create that the industry never offered?

**How to Use:** Map value curves, identify new value propositions

**Best For:** Market strategy, competitive differentiation, innovation

---

### McKinsey's Three Horizons
**Purpose:** Balance short-term performance with long-term innovation

**Three Horizons:**
- **Horizon 1: Core Business** - Maximize current profits and stability (0-2 years)
- **Horizon 2: Emerging Opportunities** - Develop next growth engines (1-3 years)
- **Horizon 3: Future Ideas** - R&D and long-term innovations (3-5+ years)

**How to Use:** Allocate resources across all three horizons to balance present and future

**Best For:** Portfolio management, innovation planning, growth strategy

---

### Hambrick & Fredrickson's Strategy Diamond
**Purpose:** Comprehensive strategy definition across five dimensions

**Five Facets:**
- **Arenas:** Where will we be active? (categories, channels, geographies, technologies)
- **Vehicles:** How will we get there? (organic, M&A, partnerships, licensing)
- **Differentiators:** How will we win? (price, quality, customization, brand)
- **Staging:** What's our speed & sequence? (expansion pace, initiative order)
- **Economic Logic:** How will we obtain returns? (scale, premium pricing, efficiency)

**How to Use:** Answer all five questions to build coherent strategy

**Best For:** Strategic planning, business model design, strategic clarity

---

### Four Pillars of Winning Strategy
**Purpose:** Ensure strategic balance across critical dimensions

**Four Pillars:**
- **Direction:** Vision, purpose, where to play
- **Advantage:** Unique capabilities and sustainable differentiation
- **Demand:** Real customer need in defined segment
- **Economics:** Profit model and scalable resource plan

**Risk of Missing Pillars:**
- Direction only: Great product, no buyers
- Demand only: Profitable but commoditized
- Advantage only: Profitable but aimless
- Economics only: Compelling but unsustainable

**Sweet Spot:** All four pillars converge = Winning Strategy

**Best For:** Strategy validation, holistic planning, gap analysis

## Prioritization & Time Management

### Eisenhower Matrix
**Purpose:** Quick prioritization based on urgency vs importance

**Four Quadrants:**
- **Do (Urgent + Important):** Crises, deadlines, emergencies
- **Schedule (Not Urgent + Important):** Strategic work, prevention, planning
- **Delegate (Urgent + Not Important):** Time-sensitive low-value tasks
- **Delete (Not Urgent + Not Important):** Time-wasters, distractions

**How to Use:** Categorize all tasks, focus on Schedule quadrant

**Best For:** Daily planning, task triage, focus management

---

### Time Blocking
**Purpose:** Allocate specific time slots for focused work

**Structure:**
- Morning: Deep work blocks
- Midday: Meetings, collaboration
- Afternoon: Email, admin, second deep work block

**Benefits:** Reduces context switching, enhances focus

**Best For:** People who need structure, knowledge workers, creators

---

### 3-3-3 Method
**Purpose:** Balance deep work, urgency, and maintenance

**Daily Structure:**
- **3 hours:** Deep work on high-priority projects
- **3 urgent tasks:** Time-sensitive matters
- **3 maintenance tasks:** Routine activities

**Best For:** Daily planning, balanced workload, productivity rhythm

---

### ABCDE Method
**Purpose:** Hierarchical task prioritization by consequences

**Hierarchy:**
- **A - Highest Priority:** Critical tasks with serious consequences
- **B - Important Today:** Should be done, moderate consequences
- **C - Nice to Do:** Low or no consequences
- **D - Delegate:** Can be handled by others
- **E - Eliminate:** Remove from list entirely

**Best For:** Clear ranking, consequence-based prioritization

---

### MoSCoW Method
**Purpose:** Categorize requirements for project scope management

**Categories:**
- **M - MUST HAVE:** Essential, project fails without these
- **S - SHOULD HAVE:** Important, significant value, not make-or-break
- **C - COULD HAVE:** Desirable, minimal impact if excluded
- **W - WON'T HAVE:** Explicitly out of scope for current phase

**Best For:** Project management, feature prioritization, scope control

---

### Pareto Principle (80/20 Rule)
**Purpose:** Focus on the vital few that drive most results

**Concept:** 20% of efforts drive 80% of results

**How to Use:**
- Identify the 20% of tasks yielding 80% of value
- Eliminate or minimize the 80% yielding only 20%

**Best For:** Impact maximization, efficiency, focus

## Problem-Solving Methodologies

### 5 Whys Method
**Purpose:** Root cause analysis through iterative questioning

**Process:**
1. State the problem
2. Ask "Why?" and answer
3. Ask "Why?" of that answer
4. Repeat 5 times to reach root cause
5. Focus on systems, not blame

**Example:**
- Problem: New hires quit after 2 weeks
- Why? → Felt overwhelmed
- Why? → No one helped them
- Why? → Colleagues didn't know they should
- Why? → No onboarding plan (ROOT CAUSE)

**Best For:** Operational issues, process failures, systemic problems

---

### Cynefin Framework
**Purpose:** Match problem-solving approach to problem complexity

**Four Domains:**
- **Clear:** Obvious issue → Use standard procedures
- **Complicated:** Needs expertise → Get expert advice, analyze
- **Complex:** No right answer → Experiment, probe-sense-respond
- **Chaotic:** Crisis mode → Stop bleeding first, then stabilize

**How to Use:** Categorize problem type, then apply appropriate method

**Best For:** Choosing the right approach, leadership decisions

---

### First Principles Thinking
**Purpose:** Break problems to fundamental truths, rebuild from scratch

**Five Steps:**
1. **Define the problem:** What exactly are we solving?
2. **Break into basics:** What are the hard facts?
3. **Question assumptions:** What "rules" are we blindly following?
4. **Rebuild from scratch:** How would I solve this from zero?
5. **Test and iterate:** Try, learn, refine

**Best For:** Innovation, challenging status quo, novel solutions

**Famous Example:** Elon Musk on battery costs - questioned "batteries are expensive," broke down raw material costs, rebuilt assumption

---

### DMAIC (Six Sigma)
**Purpose:** Data-driven process improvement

**Five Phases:**
- **D - Define:** Issue, goals, boundaries clearly stated
- **M - Measure:** Current performance baseline
- **A - Analyze:** Root causes from data
- **I - Improve:** Fix the weak steps
- **C - Control:** Sustain improvements with checks

**Best For:** Process optimization, quality improvement, operational excellence

---

### Design Thinking
**Purpose:** Human-centered innovation process

**Five Phases:**
1. **Empathize:** Understand users, feel the problem
2. **Define:** Frame the problem worth solving
3. **Ideate:** Explore ideas beyond the obvious
4. **Prototype:** Test ideas in small, real-world settings
5. **Test:** Gather feedback, refine with specifics

**Best For:** User-centered design, product innovation, creative problem-solving

## Business Analysis Models

### Porter's Five Forces
**Purpose:** Analyze competitive environment and industry attractiveness

**Five Forces:**
1. **Supplier Power:** Influence on pricing and terms
2. **Buyer Power:** Customer impact on pricing and demand
3. **Competitive Rivalry:** Intensity of competition
4. **Threat of Substitutes:** Ease of switching to alternatives
5. **Threat of New Entrants:** Barriers to market entry

**How to Use:** Assess each force's strength, identify industry dynamics

**Best For:** Industry analysis, competitive strategy, market entry decisions

---

### TAM - SAM - SOM
**Purpose:** Market sizing and opportunity assessment

**Three Levels:**
- **TAM (Total Addressable Market):** How big is the total market?
- **SAM (Serviceable Available Market):** Market segment you could reach?
- **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market):** Portion you can realistically capture?

**Visual:** Nested circles (TAM largest, SAM medium, SOM smallest)

**Best For:** Business planning, investor pitches, go-to-market strategy

---

### Ohmae's 3 C's
**Purpose:** Strategic balance between company, customer, and competitor

**Three C's:**
- **Company:** Your distinctive capabilities and resources
- **Customer:** Their needs, preferences, pain points
- **Competitor:** Their offerings, strengths, weaknesses

**Key Insight:** Balance all three - avoid singular focus

**Best For:** Strategic positioning, competitive analysis, market strategy

---

### GE-McKinsey Nine-Box Matrix
**Purpose:** Portfolio analysis and resource allocation

**Structure:** 3x3 matrix
- **Y-axis:** Industry Attractiveness (High/Medium/Low)
- **X-axis:** Business Unit Strength (High/Medium/Low)

**Strategic Recommendations:**
- High-High: Invest/Grow
- High-Medium: Selective Investment
- Medium-High: Protect & Refocus
- Low-Low: Harvest/Divest

**Best For:** Portfolio management, resource allocation, divestment decisions

## Action & Execution Frameworks

### SMART Goals
**Purpose:** Set clear, achievable objectives

**Criteria:**
- **S - Specific:** Focus on one clear goal
- **M - Measurable:** Track progress easily
- **A - Achievable:** Set realistic targets
- **R - Relevant:** Make it meaningful
- **T - Time-bound:** Set clear deadline

**Best For:** Goal setting, project planning, performance management

---

### Transtheoretical Model (Stages of Change)
**Purpose:** Understand readiness for change

**Six Stages:**
1. **Precontemplation:** Not thinking about change
2. **Contemplation:** Recognizing there's a problem
3. **Preparation:** Getting ready to act
4. **Action:** Making real behavior change
5. **Maintenance:** Sustaining change long-term
6. **Relapse:** Slipping back (normal part of process)

**Best For:** Change management, habit formation, behavior change

---

### WOOP Method
**Purpose:** Mental contrasting and implementation planning

**Framework:**
- **W - Wish:** Pick a goal you care about
- **O - Outcome:** Visualize success
- **O - Obstacle:** Identify what might block you
- **P - Plan:** If-then plan for obstacles

**Example:** "If [obstacle occurs], then I will [specific action]"

**Best For:** Goal achievement, obstacle planning, implementation intentions

---

### Rubicon Model of Action Phases
**Purpose:** Move from deliberation to committed action

**Four Phases:**
1. **Pre-decisional (Motivation):** Thinking about options
2. **Pre-actional (Volition):** Planning how to start
3. **Actional (Volition):** Executing the commitment
4. **Post-actional (Motivation):** Evaluating outcomes

**Key Insight:** "Crossing the Rubicon" = commitment point between phases 1 and 2

**Best For:** Overcoming indecision, commitment, action initiation

---

### Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) Triangle
**Purpose:** Understand thought-emotion-action connections

**Three Components:**
- **Thoughts:** Interpretations and beliefs
- **Feelings:** Emotional responses
- **Behaviors:** Actions taken

**Key Insight:** Each influences the others - change one to change all

**Best For:** Breaking negative patterns, behavior change, mental barriers

---

### ABC Model (Rational Emotive Behavior Therapy)
**Purpose:** Identify cognitive distortions blocking action

**Framework:**
- **A - Activating Event:** Something happens
- **B - Belief:** Your interpretation (often automatic)
- **C - Consequence:** How you feel and act as a result

**Key Insight:** It's not the event (A) that stops you, it's your belief about it (B)

**Best For:** Overcoming mental blocks, reframing, limiting beliefs

## Usage Guidelines

### For Strategic Decisions:
1. Start with **Cynefin** to categorize problem type
2. Apply **Strategy Diamond** or **Four Pillars** for comprehensive planning
3. Use **VRIO** or **Porter's Five Forces** for competitive analysis
4. Validate with **Blue Ocean** thinking for differentiation

### For Execution:
1. Set goals with **SMART**
2. Prioritize with **Eisenhower** or **Pareto**
3. Manage time with **Time Blocking** or **3-3-3**
4. Overcome blocks with **WOOP** or **ABC Model**

### For Problem-Solving:
1. Categorize with **Cynefin**
2. Find root cause with **5 Whys**
3. Innovate with **First Principles** or **Design Thinking**
4. Improve processes with **DMAIC**
